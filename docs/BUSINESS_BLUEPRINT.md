# Aegis Atlas Business Blueprint

Assumptions used while you were asleep:

- Skills/tools: web development, AI prompting, basic product demos, willingness to learn public-sector validation.
- 90-day goal: create a standout final-year project that can become a credible startup prototype.
- Initial market: universities, civic hackathons, emergency-management training programs, climate resilience teams.

## Phase 1: Ideation

**Mission:** Turn the project from "cool dashboard" into a sharp wedge: offline-first disaster response planning for teams that cannot rely on perfect data or perfect internet.

✅ Actions:

1. Define the beachhead user: emergency-management students, civic innovation labs, and local resilience officers.
2. Write the one-line promise: "From messy field notes to an explainable incident action plan in minutes."
3. Pick three disaster scenarios for demos: flood, wildfire, heatwave.
4. Map the emotional hook: speed, trust, equity, and local reliability.
5. Identify the first killer workflow: paste field intel, simulate risk, export action plan.
6. Decide what not to build yet: live emergency authority, real dispatch, medical diagnosis.

**Tools:** ChatGPT, Perplexity or web search, Miro/FigJam, Google Docs, GitHub.

**Duration:** 2-4 days.

**KPIs:** 10 user conversations scheduled, 3 clear demo scenarios, 1 landing narrative, 1 problem statement people repeat back correctly.

**Founder tip:** The best startup ideas sound narrow at first. Narrow is how you become real.

## Phase 2: Validation

**Mission:** Prove the pain is real before adding complexity.

✅ Actions:

1. Interview emergency-management faculty, civil engineering professors, student disaster clubs, and local NGO operators.
2. Show a clickable demo, not slides.
3. Ask where their current planning process breaks: data, speed, trust, coordination, reporting.
4. Measure if they ask for the export, the simulation assumptions, or real-data integration.
5. Run a tabletop exercise with before/after comparison.
6. Collect objections and rank them by deal-killer severity.

**Tools:** Aegis Atlas demo, Google Forms, Notion CRM, Loom, Calendly, Zoom.

**Duration:** 1-2 weeks.

**KPIs:** 20 interviews, 5 demo replays, 3 written testimonials, 2 pilot invitations, 1 repeated must-have feature.

**Founder tip:** Praise is cheap. A second meeting, data access, or a pilot intro is signal.

## Phase 3: MVP

**Mission:** Convert the prototype into a pilotable product with one real city, one real workflow, and one trusted export.

✅ Actions:

1. Replace synthetic zones with open GIS data for one city.
2. Add scenario import/export so pilots can replay exercises.
3. Add official incident-command report templates.
4. Add audit explanations for every recommendation.
5. Add role-based views: planner, field lead, professor/evaluator.
6. Add accessibility checks and offline install polish.

**Tools:** React, TypeScript, MapLibre or Leaflet, OpenStreetMap, public flood/fire/heat datasets, Supabase or SQLite, Playwright.

**Duration:** 3-5 weeks.

**KPIs:** 1 city dataset imported, 3 end-to-end tabletop exercises, 90% task completion in demo, under 5 minutes to export a plan, zero critical bugs in pilot flow.

**Founder tip:** Do not pitch "AI." Pitch the moment the user gets their life back.

## Phase 4: Launch

**Mission:** Make the product impossible to ignore in the student and civic-tech ecosystem.

✅ Actions:

1. Publish a 90-second demo video with a field-note-to-plan transformation.
2. Launch on GitHub with tests, docs, screenshots, and a clear safety boundary.
3. Submit to climate-tech, civic-tech, and disaster-tech hackathons.
4. Run live demos for professors and final-year project evaluators.
5. Create a pilot kit for universities: exercise script, sample data, scoring rubric.
6. Write a technical blog post explaining the deterministic engine and equity guardrail.

**Tools:** GitHub, LinkedIn, X, YouTube, Devpost, Product Hunt, Hashnode, Canva/Figma.

**Duration:** 2-3 weeks.

**KPIs:** 1,000 demo views, 100 GitHub stars or equivalent saves, 10 inbound conversations, 3 pilot leads, 1 competition submission.

**Founder tip:** Distribution starts before launch. Build in public with receipts.

## Phase 5: Scale

**Mission:** Move from impressive prototype to institution-grade resilience platform.

✅ Actions:

1. Add multi-city data packs and climate-specific modules.
2. Build integrations for weather, sensors, shelters, hospitals, and asset registries.
3. Add secure team workspaces, audit logs, and signed reports.
4. Create paid training simulations for universities and municipalities.
5. Partner with civil engineering, public policy, and emergency-management programs.
6. Pursue grants, civic innovation funds, and climate adaptation accelerators.

**Tools:** Cloudflare, Supabase/Postgres, MapLibre, OpenTelemetry, Sentry, SOC2-ready auth, grant databases, CRM.

**Duration:** 3-9 months.

**KPIs:** 5 pilots, 2 paid customers, 80% weekly active pilot retention, under 2 seconds scenario recompute, 30% faster tabletop planning in measured exercises.

**Founder tip:** In serious markets, trust compounds slower than hype but lasts much longer.

✅ Publish a short technical blog.

Week 3:

✅ Run a tabletop exercise with classmates.

Week 4:

✅ Build a pilot kit for faculty.  
✅ Add audit explanations per recommendation.

✅ Prepare the final presentation deck.
