# Pitch

## One-Liner

Aegis Atlas is an offline-first AI command center that turns disaster chaos into an explainable, equity-aware action plan in minutes.

## The Problem

Emergency teams often face fragmented data, overloaded staff, broken communications, and unclear resource tradeoffs at the exact moment when speed matters most. Many AI demos look impressive but fail where crisis response is hardest: local reliability, trust, explainability, and equity.

## The Product

Aegis Atlas simulates a living city during floods, wildfires, heatwaves, earthquakes, and cyclones. It estimates affected populations, shelter gaps, medical demand, infrastructure threats, and resource coverage. It then produces a prioritized action sequence and an exportable incident action plan.

## Why Now

Climate adaptation is becoming an operational technology problem. Agentic AI is moving toward workflow orchestration. Edge computing is becoming essential when privacy and connectivity matter. Aegis Atlas sits at the intersection of all three.

## Demo Moment

Paste a field note:

> Wildfire smoke with wind shift, no signal, road closure near Cedar Grove, 9 hours.

Click Apply intel. The hazard shifts, the risk map changes, the agent briefs update, resources reallocate, and the incident plan is ready to export.

## Moat

- Explainable deterministic engine before adding AI.
- Offline-first trust posture for sensitive public-sector contexts.
- Equity-aware allocation built into the core scoring loop.
- Expandable city twin architecture.
- Clear path from portfolio demo to pilot product.
